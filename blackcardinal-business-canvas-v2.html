1 Get Your Money Right (Week 1)

Before you sell anything, you need to know your numbers cold.

Why this matters ‚Üí
Real talk: Most first businesses fail because they don't know their numbers. You can't hustle your way out of bad math. If you're selling a $38 shirt but it costs you $35 after everything, you're working for free. Start with the money right.

2 Build Your Sample Kit (Week 1-2)

You can't sell what people can't see and touch.

Why this matters ‚Üí
Real talk: Your samples ARE your business. They need to look professional. People buy with their eyes first. If your samples look cheap, they think your products are cheap. Invest here. And be honest about partner kit costs - if you're charging $50 but it costs you $40 to make, you're only making $10 per partner before you even start.

3 Recruit Your First 5 Partners (Week 2-4)

Don't try to get all 50 at once. Start with 5 who you KNOW will hustle.

Why this matters ‚Üí
Real talk: Your first 5 partners are your proof of concept. If you can't recruit 5, you can't recruit 50. Pick people who are already selling something (Mary Kay, Cutco, or just naturally hustling). They understand commission. The $250 you collect covers their kits and proves people will actually pay you. Don't recruit randoms - recruit sellers.

4 Support Your First Sales (Week 3-5)

Your partners' first sales are YOUR first sales. Help them win.

Why this matters ‚Üí
Real talk: The first 30 days make or break your partners. If they don't make money fast, they'll quit. You need to be their coach, their hype man, and their delivery driver. Text them every day: "How many convos did you have today?" Keep them moving. And when someone makes a sale, pay them THAT DAY if you can. Nothing builds trust faster than fast money.

5 Scale to 25 Partners (Month 2-3)

Once you've proven it with 5, now you can scale.

Why this matters ‚Üí
Real talk: You can't scale what doesn't work. That's why you start with 5. Once you've got 5 making money consistently, you have proof. Now recruiting is easy because you're not selling a dream, you're showing results. "Look, Sarah made $380 last month in 5 hours of work." That's your recruiting pitch. And you need systems NOW. A spreadsheet tracking who sold what, who gets paid what, and when orders ship. No system = chaos at scale.

6 Run Your First Event (Month 2-3)

Pop-ups are where BYOA makes real money. Start small.

Why this matters ‚Üí
Real talk: Events are your money maker for BYOA. People show up, bring their hoodies, and walk away with custom pieces in 20 minutes. You can do $500-1,000 in a single afternoon. And it's CASH. Plus it builds hype - when people see the heat press in action, they want in. Your partners can work the event with you, make commission, and see how it's done. It's a sales training and money maker in one.

7 Measure & Adjust (Month 3-6)

After 90 days, you'll have real data. Use it.

Why this matters ‚Üí
Real talk: Month 3 is when you learn the truth. Some partners will be killing it, some will have sold nothing. Don't be sentimental - focus your energy on the winners. If someone hasn't sold anything in 60 days, they're not going to. That's okay. Replace them. And check your numbers - if your margins are lower than you thought, something's off. Fix it now before it becomes a bigger problem. This business is about iteration. Start, measure, adjust, repeat.

üö® Red Flags That Will Kill You

  • Running out of cash: You need at least 3 months of operating costs saved. If you're at $400/month costs, that's $1,200 minimum. No cash = dead business, even if sales are coming.
  • Underpricing: If you're not making 50%+ gross margin, you will fail. Period. Don't compete on price - compete on value.
  • Dead partners dragging you down: If 10 of your 25 partners aren't selling, they're costing you time and energy. Cut them loose by month 2.
  • Slow delivery: In direct sales, speed = trust. If orders take 2 weeks, partners lose faith and customers complain. 3-5 days max.
  • No systems: Once you hit 10-15 partners, chaos hits if you don't have tracking. You'll lose orders, miss commissions, and blow trust.

‚úÖ Green Lights That Mean You're Winning

  • Partners are recruiting their friends: When your partners bring new partners without you asking, you've built something real.
  • Repeat customers: If people buy once, then buy again, your product and service are solid.
  • You're profitable by month 3: If you're covering all costs and paying yourself by month 3, you're ahead of 90% of first businesses.
  • Partners hitting $500+/month: When you've got 3-5 partners consistently doing $500+, you know the model works at scale.
  • Event waiting lists: If people are asking when your next pop-up is, you've created demand.
